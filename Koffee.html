Koffee

Koffee was born on the 16th of February 2000 in Spanish Town, Jamaica. Her birth was registered with the name Mikayla Victoria Simpson. Her mother’s name is Jo-Anne Williams. Koffee‘s initial musical influence can be traced to her mother’s religious background. Jo-Anne Williams is a Christian and attends the Seventh-Day Adventist Church. Jo-Anne Williams works with the Ministry Of Health and as an occasional actress. Koffee began singing when she joined her local church’s choir. By the age of twelve, she taught herself how to play the guitar. The guitar which she used to practice had been borrowed from a friend. She also began composing music lyrics and gained inspiration from listening to reggae singer, Protoje.

Koffee's Grammy win was a monumental achievement in her career. On January 26, 2020, at the 62nd Annual Grammy Awards held in Los Angeles, Koffee made history as the youngest and first female Jamaican artist to win the Best Reggae Album category. Her album, "Rapture," stood out for its infectious melodies, heartfelt lyrics, and refreshing sound. During her acceptance speech, Koffee expressed her gratitude and shared her journey. She spoke about the challenges she faced as a young artist, the sacrifices she made, and the unwavering support of her family and team. Koffee emphasized the importance of staying true to oneself and pursuing dreams relentlessly. Her Grammy win was a testament to her undeniable talent, artistry, and the impact she had made in the music industry. Koffee's music resonated with people worldwide, transcending genres and cultural boundaries. Her authenticity, positive vibes, and conscious lyrics made her a beacon of hope and inspiration for many. Koffee's Grammy win not only solidified her place as a rising star but also paved the way for future Jamaican artists to make their mark on the global stage. Her success serves as a reminder that dreams can come true with passion, hard work, and staying true to oneself.
